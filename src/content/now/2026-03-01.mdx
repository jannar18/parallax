## Facade study — collective housing

Returned to the facade elevation for the collective housing project. The concept treats each unit as a tile in a larger patchwork — warm earth tones shifting across the surface. Terracotta, ochre, dusty rose, cream. No two units identical, but the palette holds everything together.

The rendering took most of the afternoon. Working at 1:50 scale to capture material texture — the difference between a smooth stucco panel and a rough-cast one reads clearly at this scale but disappears at 1:200. Scale determines what you can say.

Thinking about how this connects to the component architecture I have been building on the software side. A facade is a composition system. Each panel has a type, a material, a color from a constrained palette, and a position in a grid. Swap "panel" for "component" and "facade" for "page" and the logic is nearly identical.

> "Architecture is the thoughtful making of space." — Louis Kahn

The Kahn quote keeps surfacing. The housing project is about making space that acknowledges individual identity within collective form. The site I am building does something similar — each daily entry is individual, but the typographic system and the palette create collective coherence.

Next: section drawings through the shared courtyard. The threshold between private and collective space is where the project lives.
