## Working Name

**Learning to See**

Julianna's current site is called "Learning to See." The name captures the core concept but she feels it reads as a blog/journal name rather than a studio/practice name. The name remains open for refinement — it may evolve once the visual identity and graphics pipeline work (ASMV-3) provides more context.

## What "Learning to See" Means

From Julianna's own words and poem:

> "In architecture school I essentially gained this x-ray vision to see the world in a different way, with new context. Now whenever I walk into a space I automatically notice certain things and can think from the perspective of an architect. I can see relationships between physical forms, between people. I notice these things. I am so in tune with physical space."

And her poem:

> i learned to see...,
> the way little moments can speak of character,
> the way they do during a pause on a walk,
> the way light can dance instead of shout,
> the way it does in forests and on lakes
> and the way we can feel a landscape,
> through drawing material,
> within a moment of time

### Core themes
- **Quietness** — little moments, not grand gestures. Light that dances instead of shouts.
- **Embodiment** — *feeling* a landscape, not analyzing it. Knowledge lives in the body.
- **Slowness** — a pause on a walk. The moment you stop and really look.
- **Making as knowing** — drawing teaches you to see. Hands know before the mind does.
- **Permanence** — this perception is irreversible. You can't unsee.
- **Nordic phenomenological sensibility** — Norberg-Schulz, Pallasmaa. Beautiful ways of seeing landscape, light, atmosphere.

## Decisions Made

## What Doesn't Work (and Why)

### Too technical / jargony
- Section, Datum, Threshold, Parallax, Sightline, Aperture, Schema, Register

### No personality / too cold
- Same list above. These sound like engineering terms, not something you'd feel proud saying out loud.

### Too abstract
- Undercurrent, Imprint, Reflex, Second Nature

### Right character, wrong meaning
- **Afterimage** — she liked the feel (poetic, one word, texture) but it doesn't capture the "learning to see" meaning

### Not soft enough
- Most single-word options felt hard-edged

## Open Thread: The Phenomenology Bridge

The deepest conversation was about what "learning to see" means across disciplines:

**Architecture has phenomenology** — Norberg-Schulz, Pallasmaa, Zumthor — a tradition of writing beautifully about how humans experience space, light, and landscape.

**Software engineering doesn't have this yet** — but the same pattern exists. Senior engineers develop a perception of systems (flow, weight, seams, tension, absence) that parallels how architects perceive space. Nobody writes about it poetically.

**The practice lives at this intersection** — developing a shared language of perception across physical space and digital systems. Not just doing architecture + doing software, but bridging the ways of seeing.

This thread is unresolved but may produce the name and positioning statement organically.
