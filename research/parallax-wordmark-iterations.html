Wordmark Iterations

Ten typographic treatments of PARALLAX, drawn from the fonts in the brand research: Jost (Futura PT stand-in), Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, EB Garamond, Libre Caslon, and DM Mono. Each iteration pairs the wordmark with the positioning line to test the full brand moment.

01
PARALLAX
Depth revealed through perspective.

Iteration

Jost Light
The current wordmark. Geometric sans at light weight with moderate tracking. Clean, confident, research-lab feel. The Futura PT stand-in doing what Futura does best: disappearing into pure geometry.
font: Jost 300
tracking: +0.05em
case: uppercase
style: normal

Jost Medium
Same geometric sans, heavier weight, tighter spacing. More presence, more authority. The letters lock together like masonry. Feels more like an architecture firm nameplate than the airy lightness of #01.

Jost ExtraLight, Wide
Ultra-thin strokes with generous letter-spacing. Each letter breathes. The word stretches across the page like a horizon line. Delicate, restrained, whisper-quiet. Maximum Asimov energy: the type barely insists on itself.

Cormorant Garamond SemiBold Italic
The heading font pressed into wordmark service. Calligraphic warmth, forward-leaning energy. Title case feels natural here. This is the most expressive option: handcraft, editorial flair, personal voice. Breaks the Asimov mold deliberately.
font: Cormorant Garamond 600 italic
tracking: +0.02em
case: title case
style: italic

Cormorant Garamond Light
Serif at whisper weight, wide-spaced. The thin strokes and high contrast create an engraved, classical feel. Uppercase keeps it formal. Could read as a gallery nameplate or a book imprint. Elegant tension between old-world letterforms and wide modern spacing.

Playfair Display SemiBold
High-contrast transitional serif. Dramatic thick/thin variation gives the word editorial gravity. The "A" letterforms are distinctive with their pointed serifs. Feels like a magazine masthead: Vogue meets architecture journal. Strong, opinionated.

Playfair Display Italic
The italic version softens Playfair's boldness into something more literary. Title case, lighter weight. The swash-like quality of the italic "P" and "l" forms creates movement. A personal essay collection or literary review. More intimate than #06.

EB Garamond Medium
Used in the generative brand visual (v7). Georg Duffner's revival of Claude Garamond's original cuts. More historically faithful than Cormorant. The moderate weight with wide tracking gives it a scholarly, considered presence. Old-world authority, unhurried.
font: EB Garamond 500

Iteration

Libre Caslon Display
Asimov's own serif choice for editorial moments. Caslon is the "when in doubt, use Caslon" typeface. The Display cut has higher contrast and more refined details at large sizes. Classic, dependable, instantly legible. Less character than Cormorant, more trustworthiness.
font: Libre Caslon Display 400
tracking: +0.04em
case: uppercase
style: normal

Iteration

DM Mono Light
Monospace as wordmark. Each letter occupies equal width, creating a rhythm like code or architectural notation. The light weight keeps it refined. Signals "this is a practice that writes code." The Degular Mono stand-in. Technical, precise, quietly radical as a branding choice.
font: DM Mono 300
tracking: +0.12em
case: uppercase
style: normal
